Everything you need to know about sending shipments abroad

You will typically need a commercial invoice, packing list, and a completed customs declaration form (CN22 or CN23 depending on value). Some countries may also require a certificate of origin or specific permits for regulated goods.
Prohibited items commonly include flammable liquids, explosives, drugs, firearms, counterfeit goods, perishable food (without proper permits), and live animals. Restrictions vary by destination country — please contact us to confirm before shipping.
Delivery times depend on the destination and service chosen. Express services typically deliver within 2–5 business days, while standard international shipping may take 7–21 business days. Customs clearance can occasionally add extra time.
Customs duties and import taxes are generally the responsibility of the recipient (consignee) unless a Delivery Duty Paid (DDP) arrangement is agreed upon. We recommend informing your recipient about potential charges before shipping.
